My Experience in Writing in Digital Environments

ENC4416 Experience Overview

The course taught me how to analyze different rhetorical situations on the digital environment. We briefly watched in class a video by Michael Wesch called The Machine is Us/ing Us. It perfectly described how our devices are using us in a way, because we are so engrossed by them. So, understanding how to analyze the digital environment can help us shape our digital rhetoric for the good. We also learned what types of modalities should be used in certain situations. I also learned how to analyze the rhetoric and visual designs of websites. The topics that I've learned in this course will help me improve my digital writing and presence online in my daily life.

Rhetoric and First Project

During the first week of the course, I learned about digital rhetoric. Some key points I took note on was that rhetoric isn't just a surface level thing. It's much deeper than that and the way a software is built and designed can be shaped by one's personal rhetoric. Also, the way one communicates, whether it be in person or online, constructs the way we communicate in the world. For the first project, I was to jot down my digital-related writing habits and to reflect on it. It helped me understand what digital writing I spent my most time on. I was able to reevaluate how I use my time online.

Genres & Memes

We also learned about the genres that are present on the digital environment. Since there are so many different communities and interest online. There are a vast amount of genres out there on the web. We specifically discussed about memes and how it is a type of discourse used to communicate. Memes are a form of discourse used everyday by people of all ages. According to an article by Heidi Huntington, "memes echo an understanding of memes as tools to understand a culture at large." Each culture has their own set of memes that are dedicated to what they know or have seen. Memes are like insider jokes. Overall, I enjoyed working in my group in class and analyzing the different types of memes that are out there. It's interesting to see how people communicate not only with texts but through memes too.

Real or Fake News

The second project I worked on was to create an infographic and analyze how fake news spread online. I think that this is a beneficial thing to know, because of all the different articles and news we see on the internet. It's hard to tell which are real or fake. So, those who are just browsing through their social media feeds or reading online articles without double checking the credibility of the articles end up spreading false information. In an article by Daniel Wuebben, he stated,"viral texts typically center on fleeting events, pop culture references, and emotional appeals." Viral articles or videos tend to get spread easily because people assume it's credible but thats not the case. So, learning to not trust everything that is posted online and fact checking it will go a long way in preventing fake news from spreading. Which I think is really important to understand, because false news spread around so easily.

Web Analysis

The web audit project I worked on is one of the assignments that will have a great impact on my career. Being in an IT major we learn about web development and being able to properly analyze a website's rhetoric and it's visual design is important to know. So, learning how to analyze the quantitative/qualitative aspects of the website is useful. Also, the C.R.A.P Design Principles, one of the assigned readings I had, is something that I will continously use and look back on when I develop websites.
